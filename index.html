Rizal’s Childhood Years

José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonzo Realonda was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna. He was the seventh child of Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso. Growing up in a loving and educated family, Rizal developed a deep passion for learning.

His mother, Teodora, was his first teacher who taught him the alphabet, prayers, and values of hard work and faith. As a child, Rizal loved reading, drawing, and exploring the fields of Calamba — experiences that shaped his appreciation for nature, art, and justice.

About Calamba, Laguna

Calamba is a peaceful and vibrant city known as the “Heart of Laguna.” Surrounded by Mount Makiling and Laguna de Bay, it was the perfect environment for young Rizal to grow and observe nature. The family’s ancestral home, now the Rizal Shrine, stands as a symbol of simplicity, love, and patriotism — values that deeply influenced Rizal’s character.

Timeline of Rizal’s Childhood

1861: Born in Calamba, Laguna on June 19.
1864: Learned the alphabet and prayers from his mother, Teodora Alonso.
1869: Wrote his first poem “Sa Aking Mga Kabata.”
1870: Studied under Maestro Celestino and later under Maestro Lucas Padua.
1872: Sent to Biñan for formal education under Maestro Justiniano Cruz.
1877: Entered Ateneo Municipal de Manila, showing brilliance and discipline.
